### Rewritten "SAFE" Prompt
> **Role:** Act as a Senior Retail Banking Analyst specializing in risk management and performance optimization. Do not make definitive financial decisions or invent external market data.
> **Task:** Generate a preliminary situational analysis report focusing on identifying the top three current challenges in our retail banking sector and recommending mitigation strategies.
> **Context & Inputs:** Base your analysis strictly on the following provided, sanitized data points (do not assume these are broadly representative without further verification):
>  * *[Insert sanitized recent customer behavior metrics here]*
>  * *[Insert sanitized risk exposure data here]*
>  * *[Insert sanitized performance metrics here]*
> **Format & Expectations:** Structure your response as a professional report with the following specific sections:
>  1. **Problem Identification & Root Causes:** Outline the top 3 problems based *only* on the provided metrics.
>  2. **Actionable Recommendations:** Provide 2 "quick win" actions to improve profitability and 1 longer-term strategic decision to reduce risk, referencing how industry peers handle similar specific scenarios.
>  3. **Quality Control:** At the end of your report, you must:
>    * List your assumptions explicitly.
>    * Provide at least 2 alternative hypotheses for the causes of the identified problems.
>    * Flag what requires verification from trusted internal sources.
> 
### How this meets the SAFE Framework
Here is a breakdown of how the original "bad prompt" was transformed using the provided guidelines:
 * **S = Scope (one task, one outcome):** The original prompt was too broad, asking for everything from risk exposure to market trends and strategic decisions. The rewritten prompt narrows the scope to generating a single "preliminary situational analysis report" with a strict limit on outputs (e.g., top three challenges, two quick wins).
 * **A = Audience/Role ("act as...", expertise, do-not-do):** The prompt now explicitly assigns the role of "Senior Retail Banking Analyst," defines the expertise (risk management/performance), and includes a clear "do not" instruction regarding inventing data.
 * **F = Facts (minimal, sanitized inputs):** Instead of vaguely telling the AI to "Consider recent customer behavior," which invites hallucination, the new prompt sets up clear placeholders for sanitized, specific inputs to be provided by the user.
 * **E = Expectations (format + quality gates):** The output format is explicitly defined into three numbered sections. Crucially, it integrates the required safety constraints:
   * "List your assumptions explicitly."
   * "Provide at least 2 alternative hypotheses."
   * "Flag what requires verification from trusted internal sources."
